From hardware to code — starting from zero.

Everything digital — every app, website, and system quietly running modern life — was built by someone who once knew nothing about it. Computer science is the study of what machines can do and how to instruct them.

At its core, a computer takes data as input, processes it, and produces information as output. The instructions that drive this process are called programs — and writing them is programming.

This course starts from the physical machine: its circuits, memory, and processor. It then moves into writing real Python programs that solve real problems. No prior experience needed.
